UX Design

Reimagining the Atlas of Canada

This portion of the project prioritizes a user-centric redesign of the Atlas of Canada's Home page, informed by insights from focus groups and workshops. Graphic elements intuitively guide users, curated themes enhance search efficiency, and well-organized categories like the Learning Portal and Map Series improve content accessibility. The addition of a Resources section empowers users with in-depth information. The redesigned page aims to boost user engagement, strengthen SEO, and align with Federal Identity Program design standards and WCAG.

Algonquin College Interview Project

As a solo project, I successfully managed the pre-production phase of this interview by defining objectives, coordinating with the interviewee, scripting, location scouting, and equipment setup. During production, I ensured optimal multi-camera and lighting setups, utilized an external microphone for high-quality audio, and captured relevant B-roll footage. In post-production, I handled importing and editing the footage, integrating B-roll, performing color correction, and enhancing audio. Throughout this solo project, my effective time management and attention to detail were crucial for achieving success.

Graphic Design

GEO.ca Poster

Designing a marketing poster to be displayed at events such as conferences required the target audience's preferences and familiarity to be carefully considered, ensuring the delivery of a clear and concise message. I established a visual hierarchy through strategic use of size, color, and layout, while maintaining consistent branding elements for a cohesive identity. Prioritizing readability with thoughtful typography choices and incorporating white space contributed to a clean and professional appearance. To drive engagement, I included separate QR codes based on official language preference, enhancing user convenience and facilitating analytics insights.

CCMEO Placemat

Designing an informational placemat for CCMEO required defining clear objectives, tailoring the design to the target audience's preferences, and establishing a content hierarchy to emphasize critical information. By incorporating visuals, branding elements, and a user-friendly layout with readable typography, I ensured the placemat was engaging and aligned with the brand. Additionally, I considered color psychology for the palette, prioritized usability and accessibility, and implemented an iterative feedback loop for refinement and optimization.
